## ❌ Issues That Need Fixing

### 1. Missing Bleed
**What's wrong:** Your artwork has no bleed — the design stops exactly at the edge of the page. When printers trim thousands of sheets, tiny shifts can occur, leaving a thin white border along the edge of your finished product.

**How to fix it:** Extend your background colours and images **3mm beyond each edge** of your design. Your designer or the software you used to create the file (e.g. InDesign, Illustrator) can add this.

---

### 2. Fonts Not Embedded
**What's wrong:** The fonts used in your file (**Helvetica** and **Helvetica Bold**) are not saved inside the PDF. If our systems don't have those fonts installed, text may display incorrectly or be substituted with a different font entirely.

**How to fix it:** When saving or exporting your PDF, make sure **"Embed Fonts"** is switched on. In most design applications this is found in the PDF export settings.

---

### 3. Incorrect Colour Profile
**What's wrong:** Your file is set up using **RGB colour** (a screen-based colour mode). Commercial printers use **CMYK** inks, and converting automatically can cause colours to look duller or shift unexpectedly in print.

**How to fix it:** Convert your artwork to **CMYK colour mode** before exporting your final PDF. Your designer can do this in applications like Adobe Illustrator, InDesign, or Photoshop.
