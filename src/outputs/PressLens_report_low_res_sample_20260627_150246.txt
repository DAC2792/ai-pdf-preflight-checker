## Issues Found

### 1. 🖼️ Low Image Resolution — Page 1
**What's wrong:** Your image resolution is only 51 DPI. For crisp, professional print results, a minimum of 300 DPI is required. At this resolution, images will appear blurry and pixelated when printed.

**Fix:** Replace any images in the file with high-resolution versions (300 DPI or above) and re-export the PDF.

---

### 2. 📐 No Bleed Area — Pages 1 & 2
**What's wrong:** Your file has no bleed (0mm on all sides). Bleed is a small border of extra artwork that extends beyond the trim edge. Without it, the final printed piece may show thin white edges after cutting.

**Fix:** Extend your background colours and images by at least 3mm beyond the page edges, then re-export your PDF with bleed settings enabled.

---

### 3. 🔤 Fonts Not Embedded — Pages 1 & 2
**What's wrong:** The fonts used (Helvetica and Helvetica Bold) are not embedded in the file. This means the printer's system may substitute a different font, causing your text to look wrong or reflow unexpectedly.

**Fix:** Re-export your PDF with font embedding turned on. In most design applications, this is found under PDF export settings — look for an option to "embed all fonts."

---

### 4. 🎨 Wrong Colour Mode — Page 1
**What's wrong:** Your file is set to RGB colour, which is designed for screens. Commercial printing uses CMYK. Colours may shift or look different when converted at print stage.

**Fix:** Convert your document to CMYK colour mode in your design application before re-exporting the PDF.
