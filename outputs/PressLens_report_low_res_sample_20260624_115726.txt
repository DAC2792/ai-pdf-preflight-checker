### ❌ 1. Missing Bleed — Pages 1 & 2
**What this means:** Bleed is a small border of extra artwork that extends beyond the edge of the page. Without it, you risk ending up with thin white edges on your finished printed product after it's been cut to size.

**How to fix it:** Extend your background colours and images by at least **3mm beyond each edge** of the page in your design software, then re-export the PDF.

### ❌ 2. Fonts Not Embedded — Pages 1 & 2
**What this means:** The fonts used in your file *(Helvetica and Helvetica Bold)* are not saved inside the PDF. If the printer's computer doesn't have these fonts installed, text may display or print incorrectly.

**How to fix it:** When exporting your PDF, make sure the **"Embed Fonts"** option is turned on. In most design applications this is found in the PDF export settings.

### ❌ 3. Image Resolution Too Low — Page 1
**What this means:** Your image is only **51 DPI** — that's very low quality. Print requires a minimum of **300 DPI**. At this resolution your printed piece will look blurry and pixelated.

**How to fix it:** Replace any images in your file with **high-resolution versions (300 DPI or above)**. Avoid simply resizing a small image larger, as this won't improve the quality.

### ❌ 4. Wrong Colour Mode — Page 1
**What this means:** Your file is set to **RGB** colour, which is designed for screens. Commercial printing uses **CMYK** colour. Colours can shift unexpectedly when an RGB file is converted at the press.

**How to fix it:** Convert your file to **CMYK colour mode** in your design software before exporting. Check with your printer if they have a specific colour profile they'd like you to use.
