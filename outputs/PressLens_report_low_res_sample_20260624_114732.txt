## Issues Found

### 1. ❌ Missing Bleed — Pages 1 & 2
**What this means:** Bleed is a small border of extra artwork that extends beyond the edge of your page. Without it, you may end up with thin white edges on your printed product after trimming.
**How to fix it:** Extend your background colours and images by at least **3mm beyond the edge** of your document on all sides, then re-export your PDF.

---

### 2. ❌ Fonts Not Embedded — Pages 1 & 2
**What this means:** The fonts used in your file *(Helvetica and Helvetica Bold)* are not saved inside the PDF. If our systems don't have those fonts installed, your text could print incorrectly or be substituted with a different font.
**How to fix it:** When exporting your PDF, ensure the **"Embed Fonts"** option is turned on. This packages the fonts inside the file so they travel with it.

---

### 3. ❌ Image Resolution Too Low — Page 1
**What this means:** Your image resolution is only **51 DPI**, which is extremely low. For print, a minimum of **300 DPI** is required. At 51 DPI, images will appear blurry and pixelated when printed.
**How to fix it:** Replace any images with **high-resolution versions (300 DPI or above)**. Avoid simply resizing or resampling low-quality images — you'll need the original high-resolution files.

---

### 4. ❌ Wrong Colour Mode — Page 1
**What this means:** Your file is set to **RGB** colour mode, which is designed for screens. Commercial printing uses **CMYK**. Without converting, colours may shift and look different in print than they do on your screen.
**How to fix it:** Convert your document to **CMYK colour mode** before exporting. This is usually found in your design application under *Document Colour Mode* or *Export Settings*.
